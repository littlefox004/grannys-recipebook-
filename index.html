Lunch

  • Lasagna Alfredo

    • To make a delicious Lasagna Alfredo, begin by preparing the Alfredo sauce. In a saucepan, melt 1 cup of unsalted butter over medium heat, then add 3 minced garlic cloves and sauté for about a minute. Stir in 2 cups of heavy cream and bring it to a simmer. Gradually whisk in 2 cups of grated Parmesan cheese until smooth, then season with salt, pepper, and a pinch of nutmeg. Set aside. If using traditional lasagna noodles, cook them until al dente and drain. In a bowl, combine 2 cups of ricotta cheese with 1 egg, a pinch of salt, and pepper.

    Next,preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and grease a 9x13 inch baking dish. Spread some Alfredo sauce on the bottom, layer noodles, half of the ricotta mixture, cooked meat (if using), more Alfredo sauce, and a sprinkle of mozzarella and Parmesan. Repeat the layers, finishing with noodles and Alfredo sauce topped with mozzarella and Parmesan. Cover with foil and bake for 25 minutes, then uncover and bake for an additional 15-20 minutes until golden and bubbly. Let it rest for 10-15 minutes before serving, and garnish with fresh herbs. Enjoy!

  • Pot Green Lentil Dal

    • To make a comforting Pot Green Lentil Dal, start by rinsing 1 cup of green lentils under cold water until clear. In a pressure cooker, heat 2 tablespoons of oil over medium heat and add 1 chopped onion, cooking until golden brown. Stir in 2 minced garlic cloves, 1 tablespoon of grated ginger, and 2 chopped green chilies, sautéing until fragrant. Add the rinsed lentils, 4 cups of vegetable broth or water, 1 teaspoon of turmeric, and salt to taste. Close the lid and cook on high pressure for about 15 minutes, allowing the pressure to release naturally.

    Once cooked, stir the dal, adjusting the consistency with additional water if necessary. In a separate small pan, prepare a tempering by heating 1 tablespoon of oil and adding 1 teaspoon of cumin seeds, 2 dried red chilies, and a handful of fresh cilantro. Pour the tempering over the dal for extra flavor. Serve hot with rice or naan, garnished with lime juice and cilantro. Enjoy your nutritious and comforting Pot Green Lentil Dal!

  • Potatisgratäng - The Swedish Way

    • To create traditional Swedish Potatisgratäng (potato gratin), preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Peel and thinly slice 2-3 medium potatoes (about 2 lbs) using a mandoline or sharp knife. In a saucepan over medium heat, melt 2 tablespoons of butter and add 1 chopped onion, sautéing until soft and translucent. Stir in 1 cup of heavy cream, 1 cup of whole milk, and season with salt, pepper, and a pinch of nutmeg. Bring to a simmer.

    In a greased baking dish, layer half of the sliced potatoes, followed by half of the onion and cream mixture. Repeat with the remaining potatoes and pour the rest of the cream mixture on top. Sprinkle about 1 cup of grated cheese (such as Gruyère) over the top. Cover with foil and bake for 45 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 15-20 minutes until the top is golden brown and bubbly. Allow it to rest for a few minutes before serving. Enjoy this creamy side dish alongside meat or as a comforting vegetarian main!

Dinner Date

  • Mushroom Soup

    • To make a comforting Mushroom Soup, start by heating 2 tablespoons of olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add 1 chopped onion and sauté until translucent, about 5 minutes. Stir in 3 cloves of minced garlic and cook for another minute. Next, add 16 ounces of sliced mushrooms (button or cremini) and cook until softened, about 7-10 minutes. Sprinkle in 1 teaspoon of thyme, salt, and pepper to taste. Pour in 4 cups of vegetable or chicken broth and bring to a simmer. Let it cook for about 15 minutes.

    For a creamier texture, blend a portion of the soup using an immersion blender or transfer to a countertop blender. Stir in 1 cup of heavy cream or coconut milk and let it heat through without boiling. Serve hot, garnished with fresh parsley and crusty bread on the side. Enjoy this rich, flavorful soup as a satisfying dinner option!

  • Gochujang Pasta

    • To make delicious Gochujang Pasta, start by cooking 250g of your favorite pasta according to the package instructions. While it cooks, heat a tablespoon of olive oil in a pan over medium heat. Add 2 minced garlic cloves and sauté until fragrant. Then, stir in 2 tablespoons of gochujang, a Korean red chili paste, for that spicy kick. Pour in 1 cup of heavy cream and mix well, allowing it to simmer for a few minutes until thickened. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Once the pasta is ready, drain it and toss it into the sauce, ensuring every strand is coated. For added flavor, mix in a handful of chopped green onions and garnish with sesame seeds or grated parmesan. Serve hot, and enjoy the perfect fusion of spicy and creamy flavors in every bite!

  • Homemade Medjool Minibars

    • To make delicious Homemade Medjool Minibars, start by gathering your ingredients: 1 cup of pitted Medjool dates, 1 cup of nuts (like almonds or walnuts), and ½ cup of oats. In a food processor, combine the dates and nuts, blending until the mixture is sticky and crumbly. Add the oats and a pinch of salt, then pulse again until everything is well combined. Line a small baking dish with parchment paper and evenly spread the mixture into the dish, pressing firmly to create a compact layer. For added flavor, you can mix in a tablespoon of nut butter or a sprinkle of cinnamon. Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to set. Once firm, cut into bars or squares. Enjoy these nutritious, energy-boosting minibars as a snack on the go or a quick breakfast treat!

Holiday specialties

  • Juicy Pomegranate Glazes Turkey

    • To make Juicy Pomegranate Glazed Turkey, begin by preheating your oven to 350°F (175°C). Season a 4-5 pound turkey with salt, pepper, and your favorite herbs. In a saucepan, combine 1 cup of pomegranate juice, ¼ cup of honey, a tablespoon of soy sauce, and the juice of one lemon. Simmer for about 10 minutes until thickened. Brush the glaze generously over the turkey, then place it in a roasting pan. Roast for about 2 hours, basting every 30 minutes with the remaining glaze, until the turkey reaches an internal temperature of 165°F (75°C). Let the turkey rest for at least 20 minutes before carving. Serve with pomegranate seeds and fresh herbs for a festive touch. This flavorful combination creates a beautiful and juicy turkey that’s perfect for any celebration!

  • Cajun soaked Salmon, Asparagus & Potato

    • To prepare Cajun Soaked Salmon with Asparagus and Potatoes, start by making the seasoning mix. Combine 2 tablespoons of Cajun seasoning, 2 tablespoons of olive oil, and the juice of one lemon in a bowl. Marinate two salmon fillets in this mixture for at least 30 minutes. While they marinate, boil 2 cups of diced baby potatoes until tender, about 10 minutes, then toss them with a bit of olive oil and salt. In a skillet, heat a tablespoon of olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the marinated salmon and cook for about 4-5 minutes per side, until cooked through. In the same skillet, add 1 bunch of trimmed asparagus and sauté for 5 minutes until tender yet crisp. Serve the salmon alongside the potatoes and asparagus, garnished with lemon wedges. This dish is packed with flavor and makes for a delightful, healthy meal!

  • Broccoli Bowl with Shrimp Avocado Springrolls

    • To create a vibrant Broccoli Bowl with Shrimp Avocado Spring Rolls, start by prepping the spring rolls. Soak rice paper in warm water until soft, then layer cooked shrimp, sliced avocado, shredded carrots, and fresh cilantro. Roll tightly and set aside. For the broccoli bowl, steam a couple of cups of broccoli florets until tender but still bright green. In a bowl, combine cooked quinoa with the steamed broccoli, a drizzle of olive oil, and a squeeze of lemon juice. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Arrange the broccoli quinoa mix in a serving bowl and place the spring rolls on top. For extra flavor, add a dipping sauce made from soy sauce, lime juice, and a touch of honey. This meal is light yet satisfying and perfect for a healthy lunch or dinner. Enjoy the fresh flavors and textures in every bite!



Dessert & Drinks


Desserts

  • Volcano Muffins

    • To make Volcano Muffins, preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a muffin tin with paper liners. In a large bowl, combine 1½ cups of all-purpose flour, ½ cup of sugar, 2 teaspoons of baking powder, and a pinch of salt. In another bowl, mix 1 cup of milk, ⅓ cup of vegetable oil, and 1 egg. Combine wet and dry ingredients until just mixed. Fill each muffin cup halfway, then drop a spoonful of chocolate or peanut butter in the center. Top with more batter until filled. Bake for 18-20 minutes until golden and a toothpick comes out clean. Let cool before serving for a delicious surprise in every bite!

  • Creamy, Dreamy Cheesecake

    • For a Creamy, Dreamy Cheesecake, start by preheating your oven to 325°F (160°C). Blend 1 cup of graham cracker crumbs with ¼ cup of melted butter and press into the bottom of a springform pan. In a mixing bowl, combine 16 oz of cream cheese, ¾ cup of sugar, and 1 teaspoon of vanilla; blend until smooth. Beat in 3 eggs, one at a time, until combined. Pour the mixture over the crust and bake for 50-60 minutes, or until slightly jiggly. Allow cooling, then refrigerate for at least 4 hours. Serve chilled, topped with fresh fruit or a drizzle of chocolate. Enjoy!

  • Good' Old Chocolate Chip Cookies

    • To whip up Good 'Old Chocolate Chip Cookies, preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). In a bowl, cream together ½ cup of softened butter with ½ cup of brown sugar and ½ cup of granulated sugar until fluffy. Blend in 1 egg and 1 teaspoon of vanilla. Gradually add 1¾ cups of all-purpose flour, ½ teaspoon of baking soda, and a pinch of salt. Fold in 1 cup of chocolate chips. Drop spoonfuls of dough onto a baking sheet and bake for 10-12 minutes until golden brown. Allow cooling slightly before transferring to a wire rack. Enjoy these classic treats warm with a glass of milk!



Cocktail Mocktail

  • Fruit-Infused Iced Tea

    • To make Fruit-Infused Iced Tea, brew 2 cups of your favorite tea and let it cool. Add slices of fresh fruits like berries, oranges, and peaches. Pour over ice and add a splash of lemon juice for extra zest. Serve chilled for a refreshing twist on iced tea!

  • Coconut Water Refresher

    • For a Coconut Water Refresher, combine 1 cup of coconut water, the juice of half a lime, and a tablespoon of honey in a shaker. Shake well until mixed. Pour over ice in a glass and garnish with fresh mint leaves and a lime slice. This hydrating drink is perfect for hot days!

  • Minty Watermelon Spritzer

    • For a Minty Watermelon Spritzer, blend 2 cups of seedless watermelon until smooth. Strain into a glass to remove pulp. Mix with sparkling water and a handful of fresh mint leaves. Serve over ice with a lime wedge for a vibrant, bubbly drink 'that's' perfect for gatherings!
